Here’s the big secret: There are plenty of free (and inexpensive) resources you can use to give yourself all the help you need. Just choose any programming language and get on with.

Join a Community

Keep yourself associated with the group of people having technical knowledge, not always but sometimes you have to. Join online coding communities and attend bootcamp. Google your errors or discuss with your friends. Motivate yourself by seeing others and motivate others by being an example.

Patience

The first person that you need to have patience with when you are programming is yourself. Programming is hard and sometimes you will have bugs or difficult problems to overcome. If it's always easy, then you aren't challenging yourself, and you aren't growing as a programmer. Have Patience, boost yourself, celebrate small victories and this is very important, my friend!

Continuity

Nothing works without continuity. Be like water as it never stops flowing. Practice daily even a smallest program. Never take it as a burden of your course syllabus like 95% of students do. These skills are very rare and it is something that makes you unique and distinctive from others.

How to Start

Important – Read Carefully and Implement

If you want to learn how to code, taking your first steps into this huge universe might seem like a daunting, if not intimidating task. Here’s the big secret: There are plenty of free (and inexpensive) resources you can use to give yourself all the help you need, teach yourself new techniques, and make this learning process fun and exciting—as exciting as coding can get, at least.
Whether you’re an adult looking to transition into the tech industry, a student looking to learn the latest language, or a hobbyist who just wants to understand how software and services work, all you need is a computer and internet access to start your programming journey.

1. At first Ask yourself : Why do you want to learn how to code ?
2. Choose the programming language which you think is little bit interesting.
3. Try out some online courses
4. Focus on learning computational thinking
5. Practice with personal projects
6. Celebrate small victories and this is very important, my friend!
7. Google your error messages
8. Get a mentor (or teach someone else)
9. Understand Someone else's code
10. Attend a coding bootcamp.

And remember never take it as a burden of your course syllabus like 95% of students do.
